# The 165th Verse: A Dialogue on Purity

A minimalist, artisan-style web project exploring the profound perspectives on human purity through two distinct spiritual lenses: **Self-Reliance** in Buddhism and **Divine Grace** in Christianity.

---

## 🏛️ Philosophical Concept

This project serves as a "Digital Sanctuary" for contemplation. It presents a sequential journey between two foundational ideas without direct confrontation, allowing the observer to reflect on the nature of purity from different contexts:

1.  **The Way of Self-Reliance (Theravada Buddhism):** Focused on the 165th verse of the Dhammapada, emphasizing individual responsibility and the internal nature of purification.
2.  **The Way of Divine Grace (Christian Theology):** Exploring the concepts of *Redemption* and *Atonement*, where purity is restored through a gift of love and sacrifice.

## 🖋️ Design Philosophy: Artisan & Slow Life

Consistent with the "Less is More" approach, this repository features:
* **Deep Dark Mode:** An immersive black background for profound focus.
* **Old Gold Typography:** High-contrast golden text to signify the sacred and the timeless.
* **Parallelism:** A symmetrical structure of English and Thai translations to maintain linguistic and visual harmony.
* **Sequential Experience:** A transition-based UI designed to prevent mental clutter and promote "Slow Life" digital consumption.

## 🛠️ Built With
* Pure HTML5 / CSS3 (No heavy frameworks)
* Artisan CSS Animations (Fade-in sequences)
* JavaScript for seamless view-switching
